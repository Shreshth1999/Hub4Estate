1. UJALA Scheme (Unnat Jyoti by Affordable LEDs for All)

Overview

Launched in 2015 by the Ministry of Power, the UJALA scheme is one of the world's largest LED distribution programs. Under this scheme, Energy Efficiency Services Limited (EESL) distributes LED bulbs, LED tube lights, and energy-efficient fans at subsidized prices to domestic consumers across India.

What You Get

  • LED Bulbs (9W): Available at Rs 70-80 (subsidized from Rs 300-350 MRP)
  • LED Tube Lights (20W): Available at Rs 220-250 (subsidized from Rs 400+ MRP)
  • Energy-Efficient Fans: Available at Rs 1,110-1,200 (subsidized from Rs 2,500+ MRP)
  • Limit: Up to 10 LED bulbs per household

Eligibility

Any domestic electricity consumer in India can avail UJALA scheme benefits. You need to show your electricity bill at designated distribution centres. The cost can be paid upfront or recovered through monthly electricity bill installments.

Impact

Since launch, UJALA has distributed over 36.7 crore LED bulbs across India, resulting in estimated annual energy savings of 46.5 billion kWh and cost savings of Rs 18,645 crore for consumers. The scheme has also driven LED prices down by 85% in the open market.

2. Saubhagya Scheme (Pradhan Mantri Sahaj Bijli Har Ghar Yojana)

Overview

Launched in September 2017, the Saubhagya scheme aimed to achieve universal household electrification by providing free electricity connections to all un-electrified households in rural and urban India.

What It Covers

  • Free electricity connection to below poverty line (BPL) households
  • For APL households: Rs 500 connection fee payable in 10 installments
  • Each connection includes: energy meter, single-point wiring, LED bulb, and service cable
  • Solar standalone system for remote areas where grid connection is not feasible

Electrical Products Included

Each Saubhagya connection kit includes basic electrical products: energy meter, MCB, service cable (up to 40 meters), single-point internal wiring, and an LED bulb. This creates massive demand for these products from government-approved suppliers.

Impact on Electrical Market

Saubhagya electrified over 2.62 crore households, creating enormous demand for wires, cables, MCBs, meters, and LED bulbs. The scheme also established distribution infrastructure in previously unelectrified areas, opening new markets for electrical products.

3. PM-KUSUM (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan)

Overview

PM-KUSUM is a major scheme to solarize India's agricultural sector with a total budget of Rs 34,422 crore. It promotes the installation of solar pumps and solar power plants on agricultural land.

Three Components

  • Component A: 10 GW solar power plants on barren/fallow land near rural substations (up to 2 MW capacity per plant)
  • Component B: 20 lakh standalone solar-powered agricultural pumps (up to 7.5 HP) — Central/State subsidy of 60%, farmer pays 40% (can use bank loan for 30%)
  • Component C: Solarization of 15 lakh existing grid-connected agricultural pumps — 60% subsidy (30% central + 30% state)

Electrical Products Required

PM-KUSUM creates demand for: solar panels, solar inverters, solar cables (DC cables, UV-resistant), MC4 connectors, solar MCBs, DC distribution boards, solar pump controllers, submersible pump cables, earthing kits, lightning arrestors, and cable trays.

4. PM Surya Ghar (Muft Bijli Yojana)

Overview

Launched in February 2024, PM Surya Ghar aims to install rooftop solar on 1 crore households with a total investment of Rs 75,021 crore. The scheme provides a direct subsidy to make rooftop solar affordable for residential consumers.

Subsidy Structure

  • Up to 2 kW: Rs 30,000 per kW subsidy
  • 2 kW to 3 kW: Rs 18,000 per kW for the portion above 2 kW
  • Maximum subsidy: Rs 78,000 for a 3 kW system
  • Additional collateral-free loans at concessional interest rates

Electrical Products Required

A typical 3 kW rooftop solar installation requires: 7-8 solar panels (400W each), a solar inverter (3 kW), DC cables (10-15m), AC cables (5-10m), MC4 connectors, solar MCBs, DC distribution board, AC distribution board (ACDB), earthing kit, lightning arrestor, cable ties, conduit pipes, and mounting structures.

5. PMAY (Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana) — Housing for All

Overview

PMAY provides affordable housing with interest subsidies on home loans. Every new house built under PMAY requires complete electrical wiring, creating demand for all categories of electrical products.

Electrical Products for PMAY Homes

Each PMAY house requires: wires and cables (FRLS, various sizes), MCBs, RCCBs, distribution board, modular switches, LED lights, ceiling fans, exhaust fans, conduit pipes, junction boxes, and earthing system. Hub4Estate can provide a complete electrical BOQ (Bill of Quantities) for PMAY housing projects at competitive prices.

6. Smart Cities Mission

Overview

The Smart Cities Mission covers 100 cities across India with a total investment of over Rs 2 lakh crore. Smart city projects require extensive electrical infrastructure including smart lighting, underground cabling, smart meters, charging stations, and building automation.

Electrical Products for Smart Cities

  • Smart Street Lighting: LED street lights, smart controllers, sensors, timers
  • Underground Cabling: HT/LT cables, cable joints, cable trays, manholes
  • Smart Metering: Advanced metering infrastructure (AMI), smart energy meters
  • EV Charging: Charging stations, cables, MCCBs, protection systems
  • Building Automation: Smart switches, sensors, controllers, energy management systems
  • Solar Infrastructure: Solar panels, inverters, net metering equipment

7. DDUGJY (Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Gram Jyoti Yojana)

Overview

DDUGJY focuses on strengthening the sub-transmission and distribution network in rural India. The scheme has a total outlay of Rs 43,033 crore and covers all rural areas.

Electrical Products Required

DDUGJY projects require: distribution transformers, HT/LT cables, poles, insulators, conductors, energy meters, MCBs, distribution boards, fuse gear, earthing materials, and LED street lights.

8. IPDS (Integrated Power Development Scheme)

Overview

IPDS focuses on strengthening the power distribution network in urban areas with an outlay of Rs 32,612 crore. Similar to DDUGJY but for urban regions.

Products Needed

Underground cabling, ring main units, package substations, smart meters, fault passage indicators, and SCADA systems.

9. FAME II (Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric Vehicles)

Overview

FAME II promotes electric mobility with a budget of Rs 10,000 crore. The scheme drives demand for EV charging infrastructure and associated electrical products.

Electrical Products for EV Charging

  • EV charging stations (AC and DC fast chargers)
  • Heavy-duty cables and connectors
  • MCCBs and protection systems (100A-400A)
  • Distribution boards and panel boards for charging stations
  • Energy meters (CT-operated for high loads)
  • Earthing systems (chemical earthing for charging stations)
  • Surge protection devices (SPDs)

11. ECBC (Energy Conservation Building Code)

Overview

ECBC sets minimum energy standards for commercial buildings. Compliance requires energy-efficient electrical products including LED lighting, VFDs, energy management systems, and high-efficiency motors.

Key Requirements

  • Maximum lighting power density (LPD) standards for different building types
  • Mandatory use of LED lighting in new commercial buildings
  • Daylight integration with automatic dimming controls
  • Energy-efficient HVAC with smart controllers
  • Solar-ready infrastructure in all new buildings

12. National Solar Mission (Jawaharlal Nehru National Solar Mission)

Overview

India's target of 500 GW renewable energy by 2030 (including 300 GW solar) drives massive demand for solar electrical products. The National Solar Mission provides viability gap funding, accelerated depreciation, and other incentives for solar installations.

Solar Electrical Products Demand

Solar panels, inverters (string and micro), DC cables, AC cables, MC4 connectors, combiner boxes, solar MCBs, ACDB/DCDB, net meters, earthing kits, lightning arrestors, cable management systems, and monitoring equipment.

13. PM Vishwakarma Yojana for Electricians

Overview

PM Vishwakarma is a Rs 13,000 crore scheme launched in September 2023 that provides support to artisans and craftspeople in 18 trades, including electricians. The scheme offers skill training, toolkit support, and credit access.

Benefits for Electricians

  • Free Skill Training: 5-7 days basic training + 15 days advanced training with Rs 500/day stipend
  • Toolkit Support: Rs 15,000 grant for purchasing tools and equipment
  • Collateral-Free Credit: Rs 1 lakh (1st tranche) + Rs 2 lakh (2nd tranche) at 5% interest rate
  • Digital Payment Incentive: Rs 1 per transaction (max Rs 100/month) for digital payments
  • Marketing Support: Quality certification and brand building assistance

14. MUDRA Loan for Electrical Businesses

Overview

Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana provides loans up to Rs 10 lakh for micro and small enterprises including electrical shops, electrical contracting businesses, and electrical product dealers.

Loan Categories

  • Shishu: Up to Rs 50,000 — for starting an electrical shop or small contracting business
  • Kishor: Rs 50,000 to Rs 5 lakh — for expanding an existing electrical business
  • Tarun: Rs 5 lakh to Rs 10 lakh — for established businesses looking to grow

15. PLI Scheme (Production Linked Incentive) for Electrical Products

Overview

The PLI scheme incentivizes domestic manufacturing across 14 sectors including white goods, electronic components, and solar modules. For electrical product manufacturers, PLI provides 4-6% incentive on incremental sales for 5 years.

16. State-Wise Electrical Product Schemes

Rajasthan

  • Rajasthan Solar Energy Policy 2019: 30% capital subsidy on rooftop solar for residential consumers
  • Mukhyamantri Chiranjeevi Yojana: Includes electrification of rural health centres
  • MSME Promotion Scheme: 25% capital subsidy (up to Rs 25 lakh) for electrical manufacturing units

Maharashtra

  • Maharashtra Solar Policy: Net metering available up to 1 MW for all consumer categories
  • Industrial Electricity Subsidy: Rs 1/unit subsidy for new MSME electrical manufacturing
  • Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan: Includes solar pumps for agricultural water management

Karnataka

  • Surya Raitha Scheme: Solar-powered irrigation pumps for farmers
  • Karnataka Solar Policy 2021-2026: Aggressive rooftop solar targets with net metering
  • EV Policy: Subsidy on EV charging infrastructure including electrical components

Delhi NCR

  • Delhi Solar Policy: Generation-based incentive of Rs 2/kWh for 3 years for rooftop solar
  • Delhi EV Policy: Subsidy on EV charging stations and electrical infrastructure
  • Haryana Enterprise Promotion Policy: Capital subsidy for electrical manufacturing

Gujarat

  • Gujarat Solar Power Policy 2021: One of the most solar-friendly states with net metering up to 50%
  • MSME Incentive Scheme: Capital subsidy of 25% for electrical product manufacturing

Tamil Nadu

  • Tamil Nadu Solar Energy Policy: Additional generation-based incentive for solar
  • MSME Special Package: Capital subsidy and tax exemptions for electrical manufacturing
