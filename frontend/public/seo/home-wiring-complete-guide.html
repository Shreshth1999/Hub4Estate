Planning Your Home's Electrical System

Electrical wiring is one of the first things done in a new home and one of the most important. Unlike paint or tiles which can be changed later, wiring is embedded in walls for 20-30 years. Getting it right the first time saves money, prevents safety hazards, and ensures your home is ready for modern electrical needs.

This guide gives you a complete room-by-room breakdown of every electrical product needed, with quantities, specifications, brand recommendations, and budget estimates. Use it to plan your wiring, create a Bill of Quantities (BOQ), and buy everything at the best price from Hub4Estate.

Home Electrical System Overview

Key Components

  • Main Distribution Board (DB): The central panel where electricity enters your home. Contains main switch, RCCB, and MCBs for each circuit.
  • Circuits: Separate electrical circuits for lighting, power sockets, AC/geyser, kitchen. Each protected by its own MCB.
  • Wiring: FRLS copper wires running through PVC conduit pipes inside walls. Different sizes for different loads.
  • Switch Points: Modular switch boards at strategic locations in each room.
  • Lighting: LED lights (panels, bulbs, tubes) in each room.
  • Earthing: Safety system connecting metal parts to ground. Mandatory for safety.

Common Wiring Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using undersized wires: Always use 2.5mm for power sockets and 4.0mm for AC/geyser. Never use 1.5mm for power circuits.
  • Skipping RCCB: An RCCB can save your life from electrocution. Never skip it to save Rs 1,000.
  • Too few circuits: Separate circuits for lighting, power, and heavy loads prevent nuisance tripping.
  • Not planning for future: Always add 20% extra capacity. Plan conduit paths for future AC, TV, and networking points.
  • Cheap/unbranded wires: Wires are hidden for 20+ years. Saving Rs 3,000 on cheap wire risks lakhs in damage.
  • Poor earthing: Invest in proper chemical earthing. Bad earthing makes all other safety devices useless.
  • No dedicated kitchen circuit: Kitchens have the highest electrical load. Always give them dedicated circuits.
