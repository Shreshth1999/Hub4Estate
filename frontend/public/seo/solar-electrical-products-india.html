Why Solar in India?

India receives 300+ sunny days per year with 4-7 kWh/sq.m/day solar irradiance — making it one of the best countries for solar energy. With electricity rates rising 5-8% annually and PM Surya Ghar offering up to Rs 78,000 subsidy for rooftop solar, there has never been a better time to go solar.

A typical 3kW rooftop solar system generates 12-15 units of electricity daily, covering most of a 2-3BHK household's needs. After the government subsidy, the net investment is Rs 80,000-1,20,000 — paid back in 3-4 years through electricity savings of Rs 25,000-35,000/year.

3. Solar Cables & Connectors

  • DC Solar Cable (4 sq mm / 6 sq mm): UV-resistant, double-insulated. Rs 30-50/m. Brands: Polycab, Havells, KEI
  • MC4 Connectors: Standard panel-to-cable connectors. Rs 30-80/pair
  • AC Cable: Standard FRLS cable from inverter to grid. Rs 30-60/m

4. Solar Protection Devices

  • DC MCB (Solar MCB): 600V/1000V DC rated. Rs 300-800. Brands: Havells, Schneider, ABB
  • DC Isolator: Rs 500-1,500. For safe disconnection of DC circuits
  • ACDB (AC Distribution Board): Rs 1,500-4,000. Houses AC MCBs, RCCB, SPD
  • DCDB (DC Distribution Board): Rs 1,500-3,500. Houses DC MCBs, fuses, disconnectors
  • Surge Protection Device (SPD): Rs 1,000-3,000. Protects from lightning and surges
  • Lightning Arrestor: Rs 2,000-5,000. Essential for rooftop installations

5. Earthing Kit

Proper earthing is mandatory for solar installations. A typical solar earthing kit (2 earth pits) costs Rs 3,000-8,000 including copper earth rod, earth wire, earth compound, and clamps.
