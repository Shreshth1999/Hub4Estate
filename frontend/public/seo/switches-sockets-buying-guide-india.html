Types of Switches in India

Modular Switches (Current Standard)

Modular switches are the current standard in India. They consist of individual modules (switch, socket, dimmer, etc.) that snap into a grid plate, then covered by a front plate. Benefits: customizable configuration, compact, modern design, easy replacement of individual modules.

Classic/Piano Switches (Legacy)

Old-style larger switches still found in older buildings. Being phased out in new construction. Not recommended for new installations.

Smart/WiFi Switches

Internet-connected switches controllable via smartphone app, voice assistants (Alexa, Google), and automation routines. Available from Legrand, Anchor, Havells, Goldmedal, and third-party brands.

Understanding Switch Specifications

Module Sizes

  • 1M (1 Module): Standard switch, socket, or blank — fits one module slot
  • 2M (2 Module): Wider module — used for 16A sockets, some smart switches, USB chargers

Current Ratings

  • 6A Switch: For lights, fans, low-power devices. Most common switch type.
  • 16A Switch: For AC, geyser, high-power appliances. Used with 16A sockets.
  • 20A Switch: Heavy-duty. For large ACs, electric cooking.
  • 6A Socket: Standard 3-pin socket for most appliances.
  • 16A Socket: Heavy-duty socket for AC, geyser, washing machine.

FAQs

Q: Which switch brand is best for a new home?

A: Legrand for premium, Havells Coral for mid-range, Anchor Roma for budget. All available at best prices on Hub4Estate.

Q: Are modular switches better than traditional switches?

A: Yes. Modular switches are more compact, customizable, safer, and aesthetically superior. Always choose modular for new installations.

Q: Are smart WiFi switches worth it?

A: For key locations (bedroom, living room), yes. Smart switches add convenience (voice control, scheduling, remote access) at Rs 800-2,000 per gang. Start with 2-3 locations and expand.
